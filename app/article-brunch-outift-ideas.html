Bottomless brunches are the social event that work year round: they’re fun, full of food and drink, and you can dress up (or down) as you please – what’s not to love?

Getting all glammed up for dinner is a given, but for a mid-morning event? Well, brunch is as much about outfit as it is about the cocktails and pancakes, so take note of these five style tips before you leave the house this season.

Have a think about your shoes…

OK, your best heels might feel like a great idea when you’re on your way out the door, but when you’re on the way home after a handful of passion fruit martinis, you might regret 4-inch heels. Instead, try a pair of low-heeled mules. They’re just as chic and versatile, but far easier to walk in.

…because trainers might be the best idea

It’s also worth noting that when you’re at a table, your shoes are hidden away, so if you want to get away with a pair of smart trainers or jazzy flat shoes, now is the best time. They’ll add a casual element to a fancy look and your feet will be grateful if you head out shopping or for drinks afterwards.

The outfit -comfort is important

When there’s bottomless food or drink involved, can you think of anything worse than rigid jeans or too-tight trouser buttons digging into your midriff? A dress will minimise that dreaded overstuffed feeling, and a wrap or shirt style are classic and stylish. Opt for a colourblock version and pair with minimal accessories. Oversized fits are big news for this season: think a blazer dress or a poplin shirt in a bright shade which would look great with wide leg trousers. 

It’s all in the accessories

Since only your top half is on the show when you take pictures at the table, it’s a wise idea to focus the majority of your efforts here. ‘Tabletop’ dressing requires sparkly earrings, an embellished headband and a stack of gold bracelets or rings. A teeny sparkly ornament-esque bag small enough to sit on the table will be a welcome addition, too.

Go fashion-forward

You won’t regret opting for your most stylish pieces that’ll impress your friends and have you feeling chic. Think that slick oversized blazer, your colour pop tiny bag or quirky gold hoop earrings. If the past two years have taught us anything style-wise, it’s not to save anything for ‘best,’ so if you’ve been thinking about wearing your embellished satin mules or your new leather blazer, a stylish bottomless brunch is the best time to take the plunge.
