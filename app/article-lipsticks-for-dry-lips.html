A love letter to the 8 best lipsticks for dry lips

Hydrating formulas that still bring the pigment by someone with an eternally chapped pout

Natasha Mcnamara
|
31 December 2022
Share

As somebody who’s managed to build up a pretty solid beauty routine over the years, there’s one problem I’ve still never resolved: dry lips. And when I say ‘dry,’ I mean reminiscent of harsh desert terrain where little flourishes. This problem also gets particularly bad as the weather turns and the winter chill picks up, for obvious reasons. And while finding the ultimate lip balm now practically feels like a full time job to me, I’m also always pursuing lipsticks I can actually wear.

Hydrating

Super matte bullets or liquid lipsticks do not agree with me sadly, meaning many of beauty’s all-time lip heroes are out of bounds. Luckily though, I’ve managed to find some brilliant lipstick for dry lips formulas that bring all the hydration as well as the impressive colour pay-off.

Hyaluronic Acid

So, what should you be looking for? Well, it goes without saying, but lipsticks that market themselves as ‘hydrating’ are a good place to start. These are usually the ones that have balmy textures when you swipe them across the back of your hand, and leave more of a sheeny, shiny finish. It’s also worth being on high alert for mentions of ingredients like hyaluronic acid in the product title or on its inkey list, which can help keep lips feeling nourished as well as looking good. There are some great fuller pigment satin-mattes out there though if you’re really insistent on maintaining that bold ‘wow’ factor, but you have to scout them out well (we’ll get onto that later).

Lip Scrub

Prep is also essential when applying lipstick for dry lips. I recommend applying a balm before starting any of your makeup, to give it time to ‘sink in’ prior to lipstick application. You may also want to invest in a handy lip scrub to buff away dry, flaky dead skin every day, perhaps before you go to bed. General maintenance is the best way to banish the dreaded chapped aesthetic, as is drinking enough water and staying super hydrated.

With all that in mind, these are the best lipstick for dry lips formulas to invest in now — you’re welcome.

Victoria Beckham Beauty Posh Lipstick

When it comes to Victoria Beckham’s beauty line, you’d expect nothing less than the perfect lipstick — and that’s pretty much what she created. The sleek black and gold tubes are so stylish they’re enough to convince you to purchase one right away, before we’ve even talked about the flattering colours, comfortable hydrating formula, and long-lasting appeal. Grab one of the many nude shades for everyday wear, and the new red named Fire for that special occasion.

Victoria Beckham Beauty Posh Lipstick, £38

Soap & Glory Peach Pout Completely Balmy Lipstick

You can’t beat a Soap & Glory affordable buy, and this extends to their comfort-wear balmy lipsticks, which will still give you change from a tenner. These are ideal if you prefer a super sheer wash of colour for that ‘your lips but better’ vibe, and come in peachy hues which flatter everybody. Pop one into your basket during your next Boots visit; you won’t regret it.

Soap & Glory Peach Pout Completely Balmy Lipstick,

Nars Audacious Lipstick

When Nars’ Audacious Lipstick line first came out, I didn’t quite know what to do with myself. There was so much hype around this launch, and it’s not hard to see why. These super pigmented bullets have it all: stunningly gorgeous shades, long-lasting wear, and even that addictive little magnetic ‘click’ sound when you close them. They’re also great for dry pouts as they offer a satin-matte finish without drying lips out. If you don’t want to compromise on colour, these are your jam. My favourite shades include Rita and Lana (reds), Silvia (a purple), and Raquel (pink beige).

Nars Audacious Lipstick, £26

Charlotte Tilbury Hyaluronic Happikiss

Charlotte Tilbury is THE master of lipsticks; of this I’m totally convinced. I’m in love with her Matte Revolution formulas, which last for*ever*, but sometimes especially during winter, are not the best for chapped pouts. Enter: these little beauties, which offer fans CT’s most iconic colours in a much more hydrating formula without a compromise on colour pay-off. The go-to has to be Pillow Talk, which is the brand’s bestselling shade across all lipstick categories.

Charlotte Tilbury Hyaluronic Happikiss, £25

Glossier Ultralip Hydrating Lipstick

Glossier’s latest lip line is a far cry from its OG Generation G lipsticks, which while lovely, did feel a little drying due to their matte finish. These are the antidote; super moisturising with a balm texture, they feel practically undetectable on lips. This is in part down to the 4 molecular weights of hydrating hyaluronic acid pumped into the formula, which work to condition lips while boosting their appearance. The blood orange shade, Coupe, is the clear standout IMO.

Glossier Ultralip Hydrating Lipstick, £14

Chanel Rouge Coco Flash

An oldie but a goodie, as they’d say in Sex & The City, Chanel’s shiny lipstick is perfect for anyone who wants a dazzling smile. The finish is almost wet-look, but without being overly glossy, making it pretty unique. Coco Flash comes in lots of colours including some beautiful pinks, which sort of steal the show if you ask me. The lipsticks are a little on the expensive side, but given they’re Chanel, I think we can all agree to forgive the price tag.

Chanel Rouge Coco Flash, £31

KVD Beauty Epic Kiss Lipstick

The newest lipstick offering from vegan brand KVD, these give an impressive level of pigment for a hydrating lipstick. They have a long-wear satin finish, and are made with nourishing vegan cupuaçu butter that gives the product a balmy, lightweight feel. Epic Kiss come in 15 shades, including some cracking bold choices such as blues and hot pinks. I love Shedefined (a nudey pink) with a smoky eye, or Upspeak, a vibrant red, with more of a pared down overall look.

KVD Beauty Epic Kiss Lipstick, £19

Fenty Beauty Slip Shine Sheer Shiny Lipstick

Like everything Rihanna touches, the brand founder’s first foray into lipsticks was pure gold. These are super hydrating on the lips, and have a sheeny finish that looks especially gorgeous in the neutral hues like Glazed and Makeout Break. There are also some bolder colours in the mix if reds and purples are more your thing.

Fenty Beauty Slip Shine Sheer Shiny Lipstick, £19
