The best vegan leather bag brands

Our guide to the best vegan leather bags that won’t compromise your style.

Annie Wells
|
31 December 2022
Share

on our mission to find the best vegan brands* to buy into right now, we have turned our hand to leather bags, for styles as eye-catching as they are sustainable. We all know we should be seeking out more ethical fashion choices, but when it comes to handbags nobody wants to compromise on style or quality. Vegan leather has to level up.

When looking at vegan bags, what excites us most are brands with an innovative approach to mimicking leather’s appearance and texture (so as not to use any materials deriving from animals). Shiny pleather bags are technically ‘vegan’, but are in no way environmentally forward-thinking (with the damaging toxins released when producing these non-renewable plastic materials.) And so rather than creating more plastic, these brands are looking to natural leather alternatives, using materials we already have on our planet, minimising waste-age, reducing carbon emissions and changing the way we think about materials in fashion. All while creating a product that won’t fall apart after a year’s use.

Browse the brands below for beautiful and innovative vegan leather bag styles you can buy now:

Alkeme Atelier

American brand Alkəmē Ateliēr is a go-to vegan leather bag choice for striking shapes and innovative designs. We love the signature ripple effects and pleated detailing, as well as the crackled metallic styles. Price point, £90 – £350.

Using recycled materials (discarded clothing and small fibres) and plant-based materials (pineapple leather, cactus leather and grape leather), this brand combines the elements to create a strong, light-weight, shape-resistant, water-repellent and wrinkle-free leather, taking inspiration from the ancient science of alchemy. As a result, Alkəmē Atəliēr are the only vegan leather brand to have won first place for the Best Brand Award at Mipel in Milan.

shopalkemeatelier.com

Matt & Nat

Combining sleek simplicity with flawless finishes and functionality, Canadian brand Matt & Nat – Material and Nature at the brand’s core – have been committed to fabric innovation since inception and offer a range of traditional handbag styles in classic colour-ways. Price point, £65 – £180.

Matt & Nat experiment with recycled materials for their collections (including nylons, cardboard, rubber, cork and now 100% recycled windshield resins, whilst recycling over 9 million plastic bottles annually for their linings). Their latest designs are trans-seasonal,

combining a minimalist aesthetic with natural palettes. For autumn, think utility greens, warm oranges, crimson reds, and deep jewel tones.

uk.mattandnat.com

JW Pei

An insta-favourite, JW Pei bags are simply delicious, with candy colour pops and squeezabley soft vegan leather. We love the inter-changeable colourful chain straps too! Price point, £39 – £129.

Husband and wife duo, Yang Pei and Steph Li, founded JW Pei in 2017 with sustainability at the heart of the brand’s DNA. Creating a modern yet playful aesthetic in gorgeous colour palettes, they use high-quality canvas, polyurethane, and certified fabrics made from recycled plastic bottles. The brand ethos celebrates creativity, with designs that are effortless and empowering, and we’re lusting after every one. Helped by the more accessible price-point too!

jwpei.com

Luxtra

A glamorous workwear option, Luxtra bags come in classic shapes, with clean lines and a pristine finish on each. This brand has a real sense of traceability, with stories behind sourcing each of the bags’ materials, from up-cycling market mango waste to recovering apple pulp from the fruit juice industry. Price point, £60 – £299

Founder, Jessica Kruger, has turned her hand to vegan bag design after her success with award-winning vegetarian London restaurant ETHOS. In a similar fashion, Luxtra has forward-thinking values and modern design at it’s core. Take a visit to Luxtra’s website and you can browse the collections by each vegan material, all with ecological or plant- based characteristics – pineapple, apple, mango, cactus and corn. We’re loving the personalisation options too with lettering artist Rachel Joy Price.

luxtralondon.com

Cassuna

For an authentic style, with beautiful stitching and sophisticated vegan leather, look to Spanish brand Canussa, who are are committed to creating long-lasting bags with durable fabrics as well as having minimal carbon footprint (for every Canussa sold, a tree will be planted). Price point, £50 – £138

You can see the artisanal workmanship in every single one of Canussa’s vegan leather bags, with skills passed down from generation to generation. “One of the huge problems of the fashion industry is the durability of its products, that no longer seem to be produced to last. We want our accessories and foot-wear to last and to promote a more responsible consumption” says entrepreneur María Cano, who has been selected to join the UN Women’s Industry Disruptor Bootcamp, a program that encourages women to lead the change for a more equitable and sustainable future for fashion.

canussa.com

Watson & Wolf

UK brand Watson & Wolfe take a slow-fashion approach to creating their vegan leather bags, which are characterised by cruelty-free craftsmanship and elegant season-less designs. Some of the most traditional out of our vegan leather bag line-up, these bags are an unassuming straight-forward workwear option. Price point, £95 – £215

AMARE

For a cute and playful aesthetic, Belgium brand AMARÉ have created limited edition vegan leather bags with an animal emblem on each design telling its own story. Price point, £250 – £340

AMARÉ uses leather made from Nopal cactus in bright colours. Embellishing their styles with small animal symbols (little gold rabbits, turtles, elephants, giraffes). PVC free, their Cactus vegan leather is 100% organic and free from pesticides and herbicides, making a material that is elastic, breathable and looks great too.

amareantwerp.com

Fenella Smith

Fenella Smith is a British brand with a sense of family and creativity in its DNA. The perfect option for bright and bold vegan leather bags with an affordable price tag. Price point, £35 – £100

Fenella designs all of the collections herself, inspired by her family and her animals, and works with collaborators, including her brother Greg who draws the illustrations. As well as vegan certification and becoming a climate positive workforce, through Offset Earth, Fenella Smith has designed a range of products with the WWF out of fully recyclable material, for which up to 10% of net sales are going towards protecting the snow leopard. There are vibrant and colourful designs, gorgeous patterns and personal touches, with lots of other accessories for gifting. fenellasmith.com
