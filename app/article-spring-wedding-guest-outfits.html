Dear Eliza,

My boyfriend’s best friend is getting married in June, it’ll be the first time I meet the bride and I’m dreading the whole thing as I’ve heard she wants the guests to all complement each other for the photos.

It’s in the Cotswolds and her bridesmaids are wearing pale blue floral gowns with frilled sleeves. I never wear dresses or prints, and dress quite minimally but I really want to be polite and don’t want to stand out.

Help please! Amelia

Dear Amelia,

I know the feeling so well, Amelia! I’m not a dress gal either. I always find myself tempted to try one for a wedding but I just know I won’t feel comfortable, which means sitting at the table all evening to avoid pictures and the dancefloor, which is what weddings are all about. So I’d actually advise against leaving your comfort zone on this occasion (sorry, bride!) but it’s just not worth it.  

There are so many chic options around now that are just as dressy but will suit your signature style much more. The minimalist in you will love a trouser suit, which is ideal when the sun sets and everyone else is looking for their bulky coats to keep warm. Zara do good ones year round that come in at under £100 for the set – this jacquard blue one is stunning and will sit nicely with all the floral frocks. 

A silk top and wide-leg trousers is an underrated event combo, in my humble opinion. They just look so luxe, and with statement earrings and embellished heels, it’s a perfect day-to-night look. These Uterque trousers are a statement, but you could pair them with a cream blouse to dial them down. It looks like you’ve made maximum effort which is all any bride would want her guests to do so it’s a safe bet. 

A silky jumpsuit is another great option, and an easy all-in-one look just like the dreaded dress. Oasis always does pretty ones this time of year, and this halterneck option comes in a few different jewel tones. River Island has a similar style in different colours, and the icy blue one would look so good with silver embellished hair slides. 

If you can spend a bit more, I have Ghost’s Joy jumpsuit in sage and have worn it to death – it’s really comfortable. Pair it with strappy gold mules and a clutch. This deep green shade nods to the countryside setting, and the glam fabric lends itself well to evening, too, so it’s pretty perfect. 

Whatever you choose to wear, the important thing is that you’re comfortable in it. Have the best time! x
