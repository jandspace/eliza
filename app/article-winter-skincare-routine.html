7 swaps to make for your winter skincare routine

Prep your skin for those incoming colder months in seven simple steps

Annie Wells
|
31 December 2022
Share

Every year, the changing weather dictates our skin’s behaviour and thus, each season we must make the appropriate changes to our AM and PM regimes. Finding your dream winter skincare routine takes just a few changes, but they’re ones undeniably worth making, says ‘Skin Nerd’ Jennifer Rock, dermal facialist and founder of skincare brand Skingredients. “Our wardrobe changes with the seasons and our skincare should too,” she explains. “Of course, a few skincare essentials will remain the cornerstones of our AM and PM regimens, but it’s important to comfort, nourish and protect your skin when the cooler weather rolls around.”

Here, we lay out the simplest ways to adapt your winter skincare routine for the cooler weather, from adding in new ingredients to mixing it up with your textures. 

1. SWAP a light lotion FOR a luxurious night cream.

During the summertime, all we want is to find something cooling and refreshing to tackle the heat. Lightweight water-gel textures do the job perfectly, but you may find that come winter, they just don’t cut it anymore. “During the winter months, your skin faces blustery winds, biting temperatures and drying central heating, so it deserves extra love and attention,” explains Rock. Now’s the time to treat yourself to a luxurious, rich night cream to replenish beaten down skin that needs a little extra TLC. 

2. SWAP a serum FOR an oil. 

Bear with us on this one, as it’s more of an addition than a replacement, unless you want to keep it super simple and just choose one. For those among us who’ve found effective serums that work, don’t give them up come winter; just add in an extra layer of nourishment in the form of an oil. Or alternatively, find a serum with an oil texture like Disciple’s, which offers the best of both worlds. Oil textures can really help to “keep hydration locked within the skin by acting as a protective film,” explains Rock, meaning they’re worth introducing come October onwards. Look for ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, rose hip oil and squalane; all of which feed the skin. 

3. SWAP a full coverage SPF FOR a priming winter SPF.

Summertime is a period in which our SPF needs are focused around full coverage formulas that can be reapplied often and used while at the park or on the beach, sans makeup. During the winter however, we usually prefer priming bases that work well under makeup as well as protecting the skin. For this reason, you may want to turn your attention to priming formulas that sit well beneath foundation, BB Cream, or tinted moisturisers. Just whatever you do, make sure you wear one, as experts agree that a large proportion of UV rays can penetrate through clouds, and even glass windows. “You should apply a broad-spectrum SPF with a high factor every single day of the year – come rain, shine, hail or duvet day,” implores Rock.

4. SWAP your mattifying primer FOR a radiance booster.

Speaking of primers, you may also want to change the type you use now that the seasons are changing. While when it’s warm many of us turn to mattifying solutions that will keep oil and shine at bay, the winter time calls for brightening, radiance-boosting buys to tackle the dullness that inevitably arrives this time of year. Golden, glowing formulas can make a real difference to your overall complexion, while also prolonging the life of makeup during dreaded long winter days. 

5. SWAP your usual anti-ageing formula FOR a retinol formula.

Those who already use retinol (a gold standard anti-ageing ingredient that’s also brilliant for tackling acne) needn’t change much, but if you’ve been looking to try it for some time now, the winter is your best bet. As retinol increases sun sensitivity, “a good time to start would definitely be when you are not going have have as much sun exposure,” says Megan Felton, co-founder of skin consultancy Lion/ne. Just remember with retinol to start slowly (once or twice a week, to begin with), use only at night, and lather on the SPF the next day. 

6. SWAP harsh actives or exfoliants FOR nourishing ingredients.

After a long summer of potential sun exposure, this is a great transitional time to repair and restore, and to re-build your skin’s lipid barriers (that protect moisture). For any harsh weather, we want to ensure our skin’s protective barrier is prepared and healthy, which means ditching any harsh ingredients that can wear it down through overuse, instead supplementing in nourishing, hydrating ones that do good. “Ensure you are upping skin barrier-supporting ingredients in the winter like your ceramides,” says Felton, while Rock adds: “squalane is a fantastic moisturiser for all skin types.”

7. SWAP a foaming cleanser FOR a comforting cream 

Foaming, purifying cleansers have their place (if you find the right one), but they tend not to find that place during the colder seasons, as they can sometimes feel stripping. “Look at switching your foaming cleanser to a gel, cream or milk this time of year,” suggests Felton. “You don’t want to keep anything that is making you feel dry or tight.” Nourishing creams — as well as helping to support that all important skin barrier — feel decadent and luxurious to use when the nights begin to draw in.
