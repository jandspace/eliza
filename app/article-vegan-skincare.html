5 new vegan skincare brands to have on your radar

Vegan beauty is on the rise like never before. In fact, research projects that the vegan cosmetics market will be worth a cool $20.8 billion (that’s over £15 billion) by 2025.

Annie Wells
|
31 December 2022
Share

Vegan beauty is on the rise like never before. In fact, research projects that the vegan cosmetics market will be worth a cool $20.8 billion (that’s over £15 billion) by 2025. So why now? “We’re more aware than ever that our purchasing behaviour has an effect on the planet,” explains Laura Rudoe, founder of new vegan skincare brand Adaptology. “And with so many wonderful new vegan brands to choose from, it’s easy to make the switch,” she continues. 

Indeed, in recent years, the presence of vegan skincare brands has skyrocketed, no matter which shop or website you visit. So how do you know if a skincare brand is actually vegan? “A brand or product that is vegan will not contain animal by-products or ingredients derived from animals, like beeswax for example,” says Megan Felton, co-founder of skincare consultancy Lion/ne. “It is different from being cruelty free, as cruelty free products can still feature things like beeswax,” she clarifies. 

“To find out if a product is vegan, you can often look at the brand website and FAQs section. You can also get in touch with brands easily via instagram when in doubt,” Felton continues, with Rudoe adding: “The best way to be sure you’ve found a vegan product or brand is to check the ingredients listing and also to look for certification by an independent third party such as The Vegan Society or PETA.”

With the rate at which the vegan skincare market is blowing up, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed when shopping for products. However, these five brand new vegan skincare brands are worth checking out before you do anything else. 

Adaptology 

The brand lowdown: Launching in May 2021, Adaptology is one of the freshest additions to the vegan skincare scene. As well as being vegan, it’s totally cruelty-free, and has a real point of difference from other vegan offerings, particularly in thanks to its targeted approach to skincare using adaptogens. 

Adaptology harnesses the power of adaptogens such as ashwagandha and chaga mushroom within its products to solve specific modern-day skincare problems. For example, the brand found in a report that 71% have sensitive skin; resulting in irritation, redness, and dry skin. Acne is also on the rise, with 15% of adult women affected, and 85% of under 24s. These issues are increasingly thought to be caused by outside aggressors such as UV rays, blue light, or pollution, which are all a major part of life in 2021. 

This is where the adaptogens come in. Adaptogens are bio-correcting molecules evolved by certain organisms to help them thrive in extreme conditions (think desert cactus’ and high altitude flowers). By utilising them in their skincare formulas, Adaptology aims to help skin — as the brand’s name suggests — adapt to 21st century lifestyles. “It’s a bit like your skin’s antidote to modern living,” says founder Laura Rudoe. Pretty smart, huh?

What to buy: This really depends on your skin type and specific concerns. The brand consists of three sub ranges: Dry Spell, Time Warp and Red Avert, in addition to their standalone Hydro Pump Booster. You can take a quiz on the brand’s website to find your own bespoke recommendations. Prices start at £28. 

@adaptologyskincare

Cultured

The brand lowdown: The latest skincare ‘trend’ to have on your radar? Caring for your skin’s microbiome, which is the ecosystem of bacteria on the skin’s surface. Enter: Cultured, the latest vegan brand from REN founder Rob Calcraft that makes caring for your microbiome as simple as cleanse, serum, mask. 

Launched in August 2021, Cultured uses actives such as prebiotics, postbiotics, ferments, and micro-algae extracts to maintain the skin’s microbiome. A balanced microbiome means healthier, happier skin, and the brand is dedicated to making this happen. 

Rooted in science, the elixirs by Cultured work in two ways: to support the existing state of your microbiome by fortifying the skin barrier, and through activating cells deeper within the skin using microbiome-based actives to improve overall skin performance. In short, use of Cultured promises to lead to increased radiance, along with fresher, stronger skin less prone to dryness, sensitivity, and breakouts. Sounds good to us. 

What to buy: Cultured has just launched with a small-but-perfectly-formed edit of three products: a cleanser, serum, and mask. There’s therefore little chance of feeling overwhelmed by choice, and you can buy the lot for £135. Only want to grab one thing? Our choice would be the Biome One Mask (£48, Cult Beauty exclusive) which can easily be supplemented into your existing skincare routine. 

@cultured.biomecare

Versed 

The brand lowdown: Versed launched into the UK market in 2020, and hit over 400 Boots stores earlier this year in April 2021. It already had a huge following over in the U.S., and its vegan, clean, and sustainable formulations have now rapidly taken our shores by storm. 

Beloved for its affordable products (everything is under £20), Versed was created by the sister duo behind editorial platform Who What Wear, who spoke directly with their vast community to form their brand using a ‘formula first’ approach. Their readers wanted a hardworking, accessible, no-BS take on vegan skincare, and that’s exactly what they got. 

At the core of Versed is its ingredients: targeted choices to solve modern problems like hyperpigmentation and hormonal acne, without artificial fragrances, silicones, or unnecessary chemicals. And if all that doesn’t convince you, their colourful Insta-friendly packaging may just. 

What to buy: Because of the purse-friendly price tags, it’s easy to get carried away and pick up several products by Versed. Bestsellers include their Day Dissolve cleansing balm (£16.50, Boots), Photos Please brightening tightening mask (£9.50, Boots), and Skin Soak rich moisture cream (£16.50, Boots). 

@versed

Monday Muse 

The brand lowdown: Monday Muse launched in 2020 following founder Lune Martens’ own personal journey with acne and skin sensitivity. After finally reaching skin health and happiness, Monday Muse was born as a way for Martens to share her newly-discovered secrets. 

Launching with its The Nectar Facial Oil, Monday Muse skyrocketed to popularity, eventually shipping to over 26 countries, winning awards, and receiving countless five star reviews. In 2021, MM also launched The Juice: an accompanying serum that’s ideal for the warmer months when you’d prefer to steer clear of oils. 

Both of the brand’s products are fully vegan and committed to championing only the highest quality ingredients as true to their natural form as possible. There’s no fragrance or essential oils, and everything is suitable for even the most sensitive skin. 

What to buy: With winter approaching, we recommend getting your hands on the OG face oil (from £28), which works like a dream alongside a gua sha tool. Whichever formula you pick up, be sure to splash the super chic, monochrome packaging all across social media. 

@mondaymuseskin

LOOPECO

The brand lowdown: LOOPECO launched on Earth Day 2021, so you know it means business when it comes to sustainability. Not only is the brand fully vegan (and certified by the vegan society), its entire goal is to change the way we approach shopping for beauty in general. The name comes from its desire to create a closed loop economy; a world where everything is shared, repaired, reused or recycled. To that end, their packaging is totally plastic-free, compostable, and recyclable. 

The formulas themselves are hardworking; taking a minimalistic approach with clean ingredients that are based in nature but led by science and results-driven. Star ingredients include rosehip oil, hyaluronic acid, matcha green tea, and kaolin clay. Prices start at £15.99, and cap at £47.99. 

What to buy: We recommend trying the Nutrient rich cream cleanser (£28.99, loopeco.com), which features pomegranate, avocado oil and vitamin C, and the Detox Face Mask (£39.99, loopeco.com) that works to draw out excess oils and tone. 

@loopeco
